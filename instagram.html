WHAT IS CERENYS?

Cerenys is a prescription medicine used to treat moderate-to-severe pelvic pain associated with endometriosis. It is not known if Cerenys is safe and effective for children under 18 years of age.

IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION

Don’t take Cerenys if you are pregnant or trying to become pregnant. Cerenys may cause harm to an unborn child.

You will need to use an effective form of birth control while taking Cerenys. If you think you are pregnant, stop taking Cerenys right away and call your doctor.

If you are breastfeeding, talk with your doctor about whether you should take Cerenys. Cerenys may decrease the density of your bones. This may lead to an increase in bone fractures. It is not known if this increases your risk of osteoporosis later in life. Your doctor many want to monitor the density of your bones while you are taking Cerenys. Cerenys may increase your cholesterol. Your doctor may want to monitor your cholesterol levels while you are taking Cerenys.

POSSIBLE SIDE EFFECTS OF CERENYS

The most serious side effects of Cerenys are bone loss, bone fractures, and increases in cholesterol.

Common side effects of Cerenys include nausea, hot flashes, joint pain, headache, dizziness, and muscle pain.

Serious side effects that are less common include blood clots, stroke, heart attack, and endometrial cancer.

These are not all of the possible side effects of Cerenys. This is the most important information to know about Cerenys. For more information, ask your doctor or pharmacist.
