Zaynol contains azelaic acid, a naturally occurring acid, and erythromycin, an antibiotic. Together they help to reduce the oils and bacteria that cause acne. In research studies, people who used Zaynol had less acne and fewer outbreaks than those who didn’t use it. Your results may vary. Apply the cream in a thin film to your acne twice daily, morning and evening. You may not see improvement for up to 4 weeks. The length of treatment can vary and depend on the severity of your acne.

IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION

What is the most important information you should know about Zaynol?

Antibiotics, including erythromycin, may cause an overgrowth of bacteria in the large intestine. This may cause mild diarrhea or a life-threatening condition called colitis (inflammation of the large intestine). Tell your doctor if you have or have ever had colitis or other conditions that affect your stomach or intestines. Tell your doctor if you develop diarrhea after starting Zaynol.

If you have asthma, azelaic acid could make it worse. If that happens, contact your doctor right away.

Azelaic acid may cause changes in your skin color. Tell your doctor if this happens to you. Erythromycin may make your skin sensitive to sunlight.

Don’t stay out in the sun too long and be sure to wear sunblock when using Zaynol.

Before using Zaynol, tell your doctor if:

  • You’re taking prescription or nonprescription medications, vitamins, nutritional supplements, or herbal products. Zaynol may interact with clindamycin, an antibiotic, or warfarin, a blood thinner. Your doctor may change the dose or how often you use these medicines while you’re using Zaynol.
  • You have or have ever had asthma or cold sores that keep coming back.
  • You have or have ever had colitis or other conditions that affect your stomach or intestines.

What are the possible side effects of Zaynol?

The most common side effects of Zaynol include itching, burning, tenderness, dryness, peeling, and redness on the skin where you applied it.

Some side effects can be serious. If you experience these symptoms, stop using Zaynol and call your doctor immediately, or get emergency medical treatment:

  • Swelling of the face, throat, tongue, lips, or eyes
  • Difficulty swallowing or breathing
  • Hoarseness
  • Rash or hives

Zaynol may cause other side effects. Call your doctor if you have any unusual problems while using this medication.

ABOUT ZAYNOL

Zaynol is a prescription medicine for treating acne. Zaynol is for adults 18 years old and older who have acne that has not responded to over-the-counter products. Zaynol is safe for women who are pregnant or nursing.

MOST IMPORTANT INFORMATION TO KNOW ABOUT ZAYNOL

Before you start Zaynol

Zaynol may not be right for you. Before taking Zaynol, tell your healthcare provider if you:

  • Have or have ever had asthma or cold sores that keep coming back.
  • Have or have ever had colitis or other conditions that affect your stomach or intestines.
  • Are taking clindamycin, an antibiotic, or warfarin, a blood thinner. Zaynol may interact with these medicines. Your doctor may change the dose or how often you use one or both of the medicines while you use Zaynol.

Before starting any medication, tell your doctor what prescription and nonprescription medications, vitamins, nutritional supplements, and herbal products you are taking.

POSSIBLE SIDE EFFECTS OF ZAYNOL

Zaynol may cause the following side effects on the skin where you applied it:

  • Itching
  • Burning
  • Tenderness
  • Dryness
  • Peeling
  • Redness

Some side effects can be serious. If you experience any of the following symptoms stop using Zaynol and call your doctor immediately or get emergency medical treatment:

  • Swelling of the face, throat, tongue, lips, or eyes
  • Difficulty swallowing or breathing
  • Hoarseness
  • Rash
  • Hives

Zaynol may cause other side effects. Call your doctor if you have any unusual problems while using this medication.

HOW TO USE ZAYNOL

Take Zaynol exactly as your healthcare provider tells you to take it.

  • Apply cream in a thin film to the affected area twice daily, morning and evening.
  • Improvement may not be seen for several weeks (usually 4 weeks). The length of treatment can vary for people and depend on the severity of your acne.
  • Do not use Zaynol for a condition for which it was not prescribed.
  • Do not give Zaynol to other people, even if they have the same condition. It may harm them.
